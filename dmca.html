1. DMCA Policy Overview

Batoto ("we," "us," or "our") respects the intellectual property rights of others and expects our users to do the same. In accordance with the Digital Millennium Copyright Act of 1998 (DMCA), we have implemented procedures for receiving written notification of claimed copyright infringement and for processing such claims.

This DMCA policy applies to all content on our platforms, including xbato.co.uk and bbato.com. We are committed to responding expeditiously to valid copyright infringement claims.

Important Notice

This page outlines our DMCA compliance procedures. If you believe your copyrighted work has been used inappropriately, please follow the procedures outlined below.

2. Respect for Copyright

Batoto is committed to supporting content creators and publishers. We believe in:

  • Fair Use: Our platform serves promotional and educational purposes
  • Creator Support: We encourage users to support official releases
  • Prompt Response: We address valid copyright concerns quickly
  • Transparency: Clear procedures for copyright holders and users

We provide content with the understanding that:

  • Content is used for promotional purposes to drive traffic to official sources
  • Users should support creators by purchasing official releases when available
  • Copyright holders have the right to request removal of their content

3. Filing a DMCA Takedown Notice

If you are a copyright owner or authorized agent and believe that content on our platform infringes your copyright, you may submit a DMCA takedown notice.

3.1 Before Filing

Please consider the following before filing a DMCA notice:

  • Ensure you are the copyright owner or authorized representative
  • Verify that the use is not covered by fair use or other exceptions
  • Consider contacting us first for an informal resolution
  • Be aware that false claims may result in legal liability

4. DMCA Notice Requirements

To be valid under the DMCA, your notice must include the following information:

1

Identification of Copyrighted Work

A description of the copyrighted work you claim has been infringed, or if multiple works are covered by a single notification, a representative list of such works.

2

Identification of Infringing Material

Identification of the material that you claim is infringing and where it is located on our platform, with enough detail for us to find and verify it.

3

Contact Information

Your contact information, including your address, telephone number, and email address.

4

Good Faith Statement

A statement that you have a good faith belief that use of the material is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.

5

Accuracy Statement

A statement that the information in the notification is accurate, and under penalty of perjury, that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.

6

Physical or Electronic Signature

Your physical or electronic signature (typing your full name will suffice for electronic submissions).

5. Counter-Notification Procedure

If you believe your content was removed in error or misidentification, you may file a counter-notification.

5.1 Counter-Notification Requirements

Your counter-notification must include:

  • Your physical or electronic signature
  • Identification of the material and its location before removal
  • A statement under penalty of perjury that the material was removed by mistake or misidentification
  • Your contact information (name, address, phone number, email)
  • A statement consenting to jurisdiction of Federal District Court
  • A statement that you will accept service of process from the complainant

5.2 Processing Counter-Notifications

Upon receiving a valid counter-notification, we will:

  1. Forward the counter-notification to the original complainant
  2. Inform the complainant that we will restore the content in 10-14 business days
  3. Restore the content unless the complainant files a court action

6. Repeat Infringer Policy

In accordance with the DMCA and other applicable laws, we have adopted a policy of terminating access for users who are repeat infringers of copyright.

6.1 Three-Strike System

  • First Strike: Warning and content removal
  • Second Strike: Temporary suspension and final warning
  • Third Strike: Permanent account termination

6.2 Appeals Process

Users who believe they have been wrongfully identified as repeat infringers may appeal by contacting our legal team with evidence supporting their case.

8. Good Faith Requirement

Warning: Under Section 512(f) of the DMCA, any person who knowingly materially misrepresents that material is infringing may be subject to liability for damages.

8.1 False Claims

Submitting false DMCA notices can result in:

  • Legal liability for damages, including attorney fees
  • Potential perjury charges
  • Being blocked from submitting future notices

8.2 Our Commitment

We are committed to:

  • Processing all valid DMCA notices promptly
  • Protecting the rights of copyright holders
  • Preventing abuse of the DMCA system
  • Maintaining transparency in our processes
